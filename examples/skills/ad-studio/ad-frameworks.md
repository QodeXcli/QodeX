# Ad Frameworks — scripting, hooks, platform specs

## Hooks (first 3 seconds — the whole ballgame on social)
Open on ONE of these, never a logo/slow intro:
- **Pattern interrupt** — an unexpected visual or motion.
- **Bold claim / stat** — "Most sugar-free gum tastes like chalk. This doesn't."
- **Problem in the face** — show the pain viscerally in frame 1.
- **Question the viewer is already asking.**
- **Mid-action open** — start at the most interesting moment, explain later.
- **Negative hook** — "Don't buy this if…" (high curiosity).

## Script structures (pick by objective)
- **PAS (Problem → Agitate → Solve)** — strongest for direct response. State the pain,
  twist the knife, present the product as the relief, CTA.
- **AIDA (Attention → Interest → Desire → Action)** — classic, balanced.
- **BAB (Before → After → Bridge)** — show the worse "before", the better "after", then
  the product as the bridge. Great for transformation/results.
- **UGC testimonial** — a real-feeling person talks to camera: hook → their problem →
  trying the product → result → recommendation. Reads authentic, converts on social.
- **Founder story** — "I made this because…" — trust + differentiation for a brand.
- **Demo / how-it-works** — for products whose value is obvious once seen.

## Writing rules
- One message, one CTA. Kill every line that doesn't serve the objective.
- Write for **sound-off**: the story must land through on-screen text/captions alone
  (most social views are muted). Captions are content, not decoration.
- Concrete > abstract. "Zero sugar, 40 calories a pack" beats "guilt-free indulgence".
- Read the brand's real voice first (don't impose a generic ad voice).
- No fabricated testimonials, ratings, or unverifiable claims.

## Platform specs (set aspect + length BEFORE generating)
| Platform | Aspect | Sweet-spot length | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------------------|-------|
| TikTok | 9:16 | 9–21s (up to 34s) | native/UGC feel wins; hook in 1s |
| Reels (IG) | 9:16 | 7–15s | trending audio; captions on |
| Shorts (YT) | 9:16 | 15–30s | strong loop helps |
| YouTube in-stream | 16:9 | 15s / 30s | first 5s before skippable |
| Feed (IG/FB) | 1:1 or 4:5 | 10–20s | 4:5 takes more screen than 1:1 |
| Landing-page hero | 16:9 | 10–20s, loopable | often muted autoplay |

Generate at the target aspect from the start; reframing in post crops or pads and loses
quality. Keep all shots in the SAME aspect so they concat cleanly.

## Pacing
- Social: shots 1.5–3s; cut on the beat of the music.
- Build a rhythm — short shots accelerate energy; hold the hero/product shot slightly
  longer. End on the CTA + brand, held ~2s.

## Variant systems (scale one storyboard to many ads)
Define a fixed framework once, then vary the inputs. Example for a 9-flavor product line:
a **4-angle framework** (e.g. Hook / Product beauty / Benefit / CTA) × 9 flavors = 36
videos with consistent structure and brand look. This is how you produce a catalog of
ads without redesigning each one — and it makes the generate + assemble steps templated.
