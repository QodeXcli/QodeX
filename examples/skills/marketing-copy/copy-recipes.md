# Marketing Copy — Recipes & Worked Examples

Fill-in templates for each surface, plus worked examples grounded in real brands (Seven Gum — 9-flavor
sugar-free gum; ChinPost/EasyGo — China–Iran freight). Replace bracketed slots. Every example obeys
the honesty rule: no invented reviews, stats, or claims — where proof would go, there's a `[REAL …]`
slot the user must fill.

---

## SURFACE 1 — Conversion copy (landing page / headline / CTA)

### Headline formulas (generate 5–10, score on the 4 U's)
- Outcome: `[Achieve desired outcome] without [the usual pain]`
- Specific promise: `[Number] [things] that [benefit], [zero/no downside]`
- Pain-led (PAS): `Tired of [pain]? [Product] [solves it] — [proof or specificity]`
- Identity: `For [audience] who want [outcome] but refuse [tradeoff]`

**Seven Gum, worked:**
- "Nine flavors. Zero sugar. No crash." (specific + unique)
- "Chew bold — without the sugar." (outcome + tradeoff removed)
- "The gum you don't have to feel guilty about." (pain-led, no health claim)

### Landing page skeleton (StoryBrand for long, PAS for short)
```
[HEADLINE — #1 benefit, 4 U's]
[SUBHEAD — clarify the promise + one specific/proof point]

[Section 1 — the problem in the reader's words]      ← Character + Problem
[Section 2 — we get it, and here's why we're credible] ← Guide (empathy + authority)
[Section 3 — how it works, 3 simple steps]            ← the Plan
[PRIMARY CTA — "Start my [outcome]"]                  ← Action
[Section 4 — what life looks like with it]            ← Success
[Section 5 — objection handling: price / trust / risk]
[Section 6 — [REAL social proof: reviews, ratings, logos — user-supplied]]
[PRIMARY CTA repeated]
```

### CTA bank (first-person converts)
`Start my order` · `Send me the flavors` · `Get my first pack` · `Show me shipping rates`
Avoid: `Submit`, `Click here`, `Learn more` (as the primary action).

## SURFACE 2 — Marketplace listing (Amazon / Walmart)

### Title pattern
```
[Brand] [Core Keyword] – [Key Attribute 1], [Attribute 2] – [Variant/Count]
```
**Seven Gum, worked:**
`7 SEVEN Sugar-Free Chewing Gum – 9 Flavor Variety Pack, Aspartame-Sweetened, Individually Sealed – 9 Bottles`
(front-loads "Sugar-Free Chewing Gum"; readable; no stuffing.)

### 5 bullets (BENEFIT lead-cap, then the proving feature)
```
• ZERO SUGAR, FULL FLAVOR — nine distinct flavors with no sugar, so you get the taste without the crash.
• SOMETHING FOR EVERY MOOD — [list the 9 flavors], one variety box to find your favorite.
• POCKET-READY & SEALED — individually sealed bottles keep every piece fresh on the go.
• MADE TO A STANDARD — produced in a [Guangzhou] facility to [REAL: certification/standard].
• [REAL claim only — e.g. count per bottle / net weight — no health claims]
```
> Honesty gate: if a bullet would need a stat or claim you don't have, rewrite it around a true
> attribute. Never write "clinically proven," "dentist recommended," or a rating you don't hold.

### Backend search terms
Synonyms + use-cases the title can't fit: `sugar free gum, chewing gum variety, aspartame gum,
office gum, travel gum, [flavor] gum` — no title repeats, no competitor brand names.

## SURFACE 3 — Email

### Subject lines (curiosity OR clear value, <~45 chars)
- Value: `Your 9 flavors are waiting`
- Curiosity: `We made gum you can feel good about`
- Pattern: `[First name], a little something for the desk drawer`
Preview text = second subject line: `Zero sugar, zero guilt — meet the variety box.`

### Welcome sequence (map to goals, not days)
```
Email 1 — Deliver + set expectations:  thank, restate the promise, what to expect next.
Email 2 — Why (Golden Circle):          the founder story / the belief behind the brand.
Email 3 — Offer + objections:           the product/offer, answer price/trust/risk.
Email 4 — Proof + gentle urgency:       [REAL reviews/UGC], time-bound nudge if honest.
```
One CTA each. Short, scannable, one human voice.

**ChinPost (B2B freight) welcome email 1, worked:**
```
Subject: Your China–Iran route, handled
Hi [name], thanks for reaching out to ChinPost. In the next few days I'll show you exactly how we
move cargo from [Guangzhou] to [destination] — timelines, customs, and what it costs. No jargon.
First: tell me what you're shipping and where, and I'll send real rates.  → [Get my rates]
— [Founder/team name]
```

## SURFACE 4 — Social & ad copy

### Hook bank (first line decides everything)
- Pain: `That 3pm sugar crash isn't your fault — it's your gum.`
- Pattern-interrupt: `I tried all 9 flavors so you don't have to.`
- Question: `What if gum had zero sugar and actually tasted good?`
- Bold (true) claim: `Nine flavors. Zero sugar. One box.`

### Ad primary text (PAS in 3 beats + one CTA)
```
[Problem]  Most "sugar-free" gum tastes like it.
[Agitate]  You either get flavor OR no sugar — never both.
[Solve]    7 SEVEN: nine real flavors, zero sugar, in one variety box.  → Shop the box
```

### Caption (native, one idea, platform-fit)
TikTok/Reels: `pov: you found a gum with 9 flavors and zero sugar 🫨 [which one first?]`
(For the VIDEO itself — script, shots, edit — hand off to `ad-studio`.)

## Brand-voice chart (fill before writing — the creative lens)
```
Brand: [name]
Voice in 3–5 adjectives: [e.g. playful, confident, clean, a little cheeky]
We sound like: [a witty friend who knows their stuff]   We never sound like: [a corporate brochure]
DO:   [contractions, short punchy lines, one vivid image, address "you"]
DON'T:[jargon, hype superlatives, health claims, three adjectives where one works]
Signature move: [e.g. end a line on the strongest word; name the exact moment of the pain]
```
**Seven Gum, worked:** playful · confident · clean · cheeky. DO: address "you," lead with the flavor
or the moment, keep it light. DON'T: medical claims, "best ever," long sentences.

## A/B variant recipe (the data lens — give the user a real test)
For each high-leverage piece, write 2–3 variants that change ONE thing and pursue DIFFERENT angles —
not reworded twins. State the metric and the single variable.
```
Test: Hero headline   |   Metric: landing-page conversion rate   |   Variable changed: angle
  A (pain-led):        "That 3pm sugar crash isn't your fault — it's your gum."
  B (desire-led):      "Nine flavors. Zero sugar. No crash."
  C (specificity-led): "9 sugar-free flavors in one box — find your favorite."
Watch CVR; run until the sample is real (not a 3-click 'win'); keep the winner, retest the next piece.
```
Subject-line test (metric = open rate, variable = curiosity vs value):
```
  A (value):     "Your 9 flavors are waiting"
  B (curiosity): "We made gum you can feel good about"
```


- [ ] No invented review, rating, testimonial, or endorsement
- [ ] No statistic or claim without a user-supplied source
- [ ] No medical/health claim for ingestibles; required warnings present where needed
- [ ] Every benefit traces to a real feature/attribute
- [ ] One asset = one message = one CTA
- [ ] Specifics replaced vague superlatives
- [ ] Variants provided for headline / subject / CTA so the user can A/B test
